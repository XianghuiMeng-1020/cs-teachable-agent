
**Game of Chance - Dice Probability Calculator**

In this task, you will write a function `dice_probability(target_score)` that calculates the probability of reaching the target score when rolling two six-sided dice. Each die is numbered from 1 to 6. The probability should be in the form of a float with up to 4 decimal places.

The total score that can be achieved by rolling two dice ranges from 2 (when both dice show 1) to 12 (when both dice show 6). The task is to compute the probability of attaining exactly the given `target_score` through any combination of rolls of the two dice.

The function should return the calculated probability or 0 if the `target_score` is outside the range of possible scores (i.e., less than 2 or greater than 12).

Here are some points to consider:
- The probability is calculated as `favorable outcomes / total possible outcomes`, where the total possible outcomes when rolling two dice are 36 (6 sides per die).
- For a valid target score, you need to find favorable pairs `(a, b)` where `a` and `b` are the results from the first and second die respectively such that `a + b = target_score`.

Example:
- `dice_probability(7)` should return `0.1667`, because out of 36 possible roll outcomes, there are 6 combinations that sum up to 7: (1,6), (2,5), (3,4), (4,3), (5,2), (6,1).

Your function will be tested against several cases to ensure the correct probability is returned for different target scores.
